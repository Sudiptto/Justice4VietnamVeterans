John's Story

I had a hard life growing up. Every day was a battle as I was low income, the streets of Brooklyn was cold and filled with dispair. I was in a terrible situation and I ended up being drafted to the millitary. I had no clue on why I was fighting the North Vietnamese.


Why am I being sent?
Why aren't the rich folk being sent?
What did they do?

It was confusing but my time in the millitary was much worse. I was stationed in Khe Sanh and the fighting was brutal. One of my friends got captured and I pray everday that he escaped and was not tortured.

The Vietcong were brutal

Even saying brtual is an understatement to their cruelity. Instead of the government offering us therapy or any support, the government encouraged us to get on drugs and even gave us drugs. I seen more drugs in the US millitary than in the roughest inner-city. I was lost and did not want to fight, so I took the drugs. I became controlled by the government, I "escaped" the horrors of this war through drugs. But this ended up poorly. I became addicted and lost my sanity, I somehow managed to come back to America alive. But the horrors of Vietnam follow me and my comrades. We came back with no support, the government left us.

After giving up our youth, the government gave nothing back
